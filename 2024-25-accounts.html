2024–25 Season

Financial Report

1. Introduction

The 2024/25 season was another exciting and successful year for Nidd United JFC. With growing player numbers, new teams, and continued community support, the club has gone from strength to strength both on and off the pitch. This report sets out the financial position at the start and end of the year, summarises income and expenditure, and outlines the plans and budget for the season ahead.

2. Fund Opening Position

The club started the 2024/25 season with the following fund balances:

Fund Jul 24 Jul 23
General Fund £2,970 £871
Reserve Fund £3,500 £0
Total £6,470 £871

With more players registering in the 2023/24 season than originally budgeted for, the club ended that season with a healthy reserve. This allowed the committee to set the 2024/25 membership fees at just £120 plus the cost of match kit — the lowest they’ve ever been. This was only possible due to the surplus held in the General Fund at the start of the year.

4.1. Membership Fees for 2025/26 Season - £150 + Kit*

After careful consideration, the committee has concluded that there is a need to increase membership fees for the 2025/26 season to £150, plus the cost of match kit if a replacement is needed. This reflects rising costs across the club and the importance of maintaining financial stability.

While this increase is necessary, we recognise that fee rises are never welcome. We remain fully committed to ensuring that no one is excluded due to cost, and will continue to offer payment plans and access to the Player Support Fund on a case-by-case basis.

The new season’s budget includes £23,495 in projected expenditure. A significant portion of this relates to playing costs (£16,765), which have increased due to general cost rises and the addition of a new team this year. The club is also investing £3,200 in new equipment, primarily to purchase replacement goalposts.

Last year’s lower fee of £120 was only possible because of the surplus carried forward from the 2023/24 season. With those funds now fully committed or moved into reserves, the revised fee ensures the club can continue to provide high-quality football in a sustainable way.
